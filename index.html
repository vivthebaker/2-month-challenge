Sunday Meal Prep

This guide will help you efficiently prepare multiple meals for the coming week on Sundays. With recipe repetition, you'll reduce food waste while still maintaining adequate variety.

General Approach

  • Prep in batches by meal group: Cook proteins and prep meals in related groups that will be eaten on consecutive days
  • Use multiple cooking methods simultaneously: Oven, stovetop, and countertop prep
  • Store strategically: Use clear containers labeled with contents and prep date
  • Focus on staples: Prep ingredients that form the base of multiple meals

Weekly Prep Schedule (Organized for Recipe Repetition)

Step 1: Plan & Organize (15 minutes)

  • Review your meal plan for the week, focusing on the meal repetition pattern
  • Gather all containers and storage supplies
  • Set out ingredients for prep
  • Preheat oven to 190C (375F)
  • Start cooking any grains (rice, quinoa) that take longest

Step 2: Protein Prep by Meal Group (45 minutes)

  • Batch cook proteins for each 2-day meal group
  • Season and bake first group of proteins (25-30 min)
  • While first batch cooks, prepare marinades/seasonings for second group
  • Cook second group of proteins while prepping vegetable components
  • Repeat for the third protein group

Step 3: Vegetables & Sides (30 minutes)

  • Roast sweet potatoes and other root vegetables in the oven (can cook alongside proteins)
  • Steam greens like broccoli, green beans, or asparagus
  • Chop raw vegetables for wraps and salads (bell peppers, cucumber, etc.)
  • Organize vegetables by meal group to maintain freshness

Step 4: Prepare Specific Meals (45 minutes)

  • Prepare breakfast components by 2-day groupings
  • Make two days of overnight oats in jars
  • Mix two days of chia puddings
  • Prepare two days of egg muffins
  • Prepare and portion proteins for wraps and salads (2-day amounts)
  • Make sauces/dressings and store separately

Step 5: Package & Store (15 minutes)

  • Divide cooked foods into portion-sized containers by meal group
  • Label each container with contents, date, and which day it's intended for
  • Arrange in refrigerator with earliest-use items at front

Specific Weekly Prep Instructions

Weeks 14 and beyond should follow this same structure, adjusted for the rotating protein and grain combinations. Refer to your grocery list for exact quantities.

Time-Saving Tips

  • Use the oven efficiently: roast proteins & vegetables together
  • Minimize chopping: use food processor or pre-cut veggies
  • Overlap methods: prep stovetop items while oven bakes
  • Assembly line: prep all of one meal type at once
  • Partial prep: just proteins and grains if short on time

Storage Guidelines

  • Cooked proteins: 34 days
  • Cooked grains: 34 days
  • Cut vegetables: 35 days
  • Wraps/sandwiches: 12 days
  • Overnight oats/chia pudding: 4 days
  • Egg muffins: 4 days in fridge, up to 2 months frozen

Some meals (like wraps) are best assembled the day of. Prep the components ahead to keep textures fresh.

Breakfasts (Protein over 25g)

Here's a list of 5 high-protein, sweet, dairy-free breakfast ideas that are simple, less than 5 ingredients and can be meal prepped for 4 days.

Each one has over 25g of protein per serving and includes the calorie intake and weight in grams for each ingredient.

Ingredients (for 1 serving):

  • Rolled oats (40g)
  • Unsweetened almond milk (200ml)
  • Peanut butter (32g)
  • Banana (60g)
  • Protein powder (vanilla, 1 scoop, 30g)

Total Protein: 26g

Total Calories: 400

Prep Instructions:

In a mason jar or container, combine oats, almond milk, peanut butter, and protein powder. Stir to mix well, then top with sliced banana. Let it sit overnight in the fridge. Enjoy the next morning!

Ingredients (for 1 serving):

  • Chia seeds (30g)
  • Unsweetened almond milk (200ml)
  • Protein powder (vanilla, 1 scoop, 30g)
  • Mixed berries (50g)

Total Protein: 28g

Total Calories: 350

Prep Instructions:

Mix chia seeds, almond milk, and protein powder in a bowl or jar. Let it set in the fridge overnight or for at least 2 hours. Top with fresh mixed berries before serving.

Ingredients (for 1 serving):

  • Almond butter (16g)
  • Protein powder (vanilla, 1 scoop, 30g)

Total Protein: 28g

Total Calories: 350

Prep Instructions:

Blend all ingredients together until smooth. Pour into a glass and enjoy immediately! You can pre-portion everything except the almond milk into freezer bags for easy blending.

Ingredients (for 1 serving):

  • Coconut yogurt (unsweetened, 150g)
  • Protein powder (vanilla, 1 scoop, 30g)
  • Maple syrup (20g)
  • Almonds (15g)

Total Protein: 27g

Total Calories: 340

Prep Instructions:

Stir the protein powder into the coconut yogurt until fully incorporated. Drizzle with maple syrup. Top with chopped almonds. If meal prepping, store the yogurt and protein mix separately from the toppings.
